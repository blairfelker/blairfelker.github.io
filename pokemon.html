Pokémon

I like playing Pokémon. I like Pokémon a little too much. So much so I have an entire page for it.

While I love playing rom hacks, I also have gotten into building wacky teams. This is mostly due to my friends wanting to do tournaments.

Each team or hack has a bit of information about it and some analysis. When playing a rom hack, I play without looking at trainer documentation on set mode. Level caps are used as well.

My favorite Pokémon is Flareon, followed by Serperior, Talonflame and Aurorus.

Funny Teams

Funny Team 1: Mono-Fairy Team

Ruleset: Doubles, Level 50, all 6 Pokémon, closed team sheets.

At one point, a few friends of mine decided to host a monotype tournament. I picked the Fairy type because its a good type. The team is not good objectively, but it's a funny team and thats what I wanted.

Ninetales-Alola set up (or at least would attempt to set up) Aurora Veil so that my bulky Pokémon would be able to last longer on the field and Freeze Dry to have something against the Water team.

Tinkaton was given a standard utility set. It was vital as it provided an immunity to Poison and had Fake Out. This was helpful during my rematch against the Water type team, when I used Fake Out on the Ogerpon-Wellspring to prevent it from using Follow Me, and getting rid of the Iron Bundle with Flutter Mane on turn 1.

Azumarill was the worst Pokémon on my team by far. I overvalued Misty Terrain immensely and hoped that it could deal any sort of damage in any potential Trick Room teams. Spoiler, it didn't. Azumarill's only actual use was when the Psychic team's Gallade used Leaf Blade, giving me a chance to recover from my bad play on the first turn.

Dachsbun is the underdog of the team. It was able to wall the Dragon team's Gouging Fire as it only had Outrage and Raging Fury as it's attacking moves. Dachsbun was a great partner with Sylveon as Helping Hand was able to boost Sylveon's attack power further. Body Press and Play Rough also dealt a decent chunk of damage in a lot of teams.

Flutter Mane spawned a whole meme (bringing Pokémon with no STAB) in the group. Even though it had no Ghost or Fairy moves, it was able to deal significant damage because it has 135 base Special Attack plus Booster Energy. I could have used the Stellar tera type because I did not bring any off-type Pokémon, but I decided against it because I wanted to remove my ghost typing against the Ghost team.

Sylveon original purpose simply never happened. I had planned to use Calm Mind, then Baton Pass the boosts to Flutter Mane so it could sweep. It ended up just becoming another damage dealer with Hyper Voice.

Completed Rom Hacks

Rom Hack 1: Vietnamese Crystal

Author: ???

Vietnamese Crystal isn't neccessarily a rom hack, but it is jank enough to be included here and has a special place in my heart. Vietnamese Crystal is a bootleg, poorly translated version of Pokémon Crystal. For example, Potions are called DRUGs. So whenever you pick up a potion, the game tells you:

DRUG BAG FUCK

I ended up sweeping the Elite 4 with Suicune (DON).

Rom Hack 2: Radical Red 2.1

Author: soupercell

Radical Red is a Fire Red difficulty enhancement hack that has Pokémon from generations 1-8 as of version 2.1. It includes Megas, Z-Moves, and Dynamax dens. Trainers are designed to abuse field effects like weather and terrain.

This hack sufficiently changes the game with a new rival (Brendan), new Team Rocket admins, and a new gym leader.

I swapped between Mega Gyarados and Mega Gardevoir.

Rom Hack 3: Vintage White

Author: notsuicuu

Vintage White is a difficulty hack of White with a bit of a twist. You can only use Pokémon from Generations 1 to 3 (and gen 4 evolutions). This might seem like a bad thing, but most of these Pokémon have been severly buffed and it provides a new enough experience.

For example, I took advantage of the new Ghost/Normal Kecleon with Adaptability. This is just one example of the ridiculously fun tools this game gives you. Many bad moves have been changed to recoil as well. My Jolteon took advantage of a 100% accurate Zap Cannon with the cost being half of my health.

Rom Hack 4: Renegade Platinum

Author: Drayano

Renegade Platinum is one of the best Platinum rom hacks to date. It is notoriously difficult, and like any Drayano rom hack, has a plethora of changes to each Pokémon. All 493 Pokémon are available along with the Fairy type. Boss battles have been updated to be much harder.

Rom Hack 5: Clover 1.2

Author: /vp/

What happens when a bunch of degenerates from 4chan come together and make a Pokémon Fire Red hack. You get one of the best rom hacks I've ever seen. I'm not joking, this is probably my favorite hack. The story is hilarious, (most) fakemon are well-designed, and the game loves to poke fun at not just internet culture, but Pokémon as a whole.

Clover has up-to-date features like the Fairy type and the physical special split. It also introduces new mechanics like the AI having access to switch mode when you are playing on switch mode. If you're okay with offensive jokes and are willing to take on the Fochun region, I have one thing to say to you:

Play Pokémon Clover.

Rom Hack 6: Creepy Black

Author: ???

This hack is based off of the creepypasta "Creepy Black". The gist is that once you pick your starter, there is a second Pokémon: Ghost. This Pokémon essentially trivializes the game. Sinking Sapphire is an difficulty hack of Alpha Sapphire. All 721 Pokémon are included in this hack. Boss trainers have access to Megas, and Pokémon have been tweaked to be stronger.

Rom Hack 8: Clover 1.2 (Post-Game)

Rom Hack 12: My Ass

Rom Hack 13: Inclement Emerald 1.13

Author: Buffel Saft

Inclement Emerald is similar to Radical Red, but it is based on Pokémon Emerald instead. Generations 1-7 are available and plenty of reworks are afoot. Weather is permanent, but has less boost to moves, Shell Bell is an actual viable item to use, and a lot more. The level scales with your party, so it's really easy to grind up to the level cap. This is also a decompilation hack, so cheats won't work.

I played on Challenge mode, which makes trainers have EVs, forces Set mode, and does not allow the bag to be selected during battle. My level cap setting was Strict, so the champion fight was around level 95 at the maximum.

Rom Hack 14: Snakewood

Author: Cutlerine

If you know you know. If you don't know, Snakewood is a very old hack of Pokémon Ruby. The Hoenn region has been infested with zombies. There are a few fakemons and new evolutions. I used Qwilshark which evolved from Qwilfish. Keep in mind that this game was made in 2010.

I will say my favorite part of this game is the Deadly 7. All 10 of them.

The Gardevoir doesn't have a weird arm, the thing on the bottom right is actually a fakemon called X32763. It's an Electric/Steel type with Levitate.

Rom Hack 15: Gaia

Author: Spherical Ice

Pokémon Gaia is a Fire Red hack that takes you through the new Orbtus region. The player is given access to most Pokémon from generations 1-6. Mega evolutions are included. This hack has a great story and pacing. I found the difficulty to be fair.

My Mega of choice was Mega Ampharos

You might notice that Leafeon and Clawitzer look off. This is because the Hall of Fame at the time of playing was bugged, and I had to edit both of them into the screenshot.

Rom Hack 16: Renegade Platinum

Author: Drayano

I played Renegade Platinum again. This time I restricted myself to Dark Types only. Seviper and Luxray have an added Dark typing, as well as Feraligatr (not pictured). Aaron was a nightmare to beat, as his bug types hit hard. Especially his Heracross with Guts and Flame Orb.

Rom Hack 17: Fools Gold 1.3

Author: celadonk

Fools Gold is a re-imagining of the Johto and Kanto. Every Pokémon has been redesigned, some more different than others. There are a few new towns and routes as well. The downside is that it's a bit more linear, but the progression is worth it. The game is slightly harder, though it definitely ramps up for the Elite Four.

This hack is one of my favorites, simply because it does all of the fakemon really well.

Each Pokémon is named after the Stands in JoJo's Bizzare Adventure.

Rom Hack 18: Fools Gold 1.3 (Post-Game)

Author: celadonk

The post-game takes place in the Sevii Islands. Along with Kantonian forms, Seviian forms become available as well. Hoenn Pokémon are introduced as well, keeping the game fresh. The story here has you go through each island defeating the guardians. Once you defeat the final boss in Seven Island, the game is complete.

I ran out of Stands since I completed the Pokédex, so I ended up making references to JJBA.

Rom Hack 19: Black 2 Kaizo

Author: sauceyaTTa

As the name implies, Black 2 Kaizo is a Kaizo hack of Black 2. It is intended to be very hard. Each trainer is given competitive teams. Also, many items are guarded by trainers to create more progression in the game. To combat this, the Pokémon Center's top floor has extra encounters for the player and abilities have been changed (Sap Sipper Swampert my beloved).

This hack is also an all-doubles hack.

Rom Hack 21: Unbound

Author: Skeli

Unbound is a great rom hack of Fire Red, taking you the Borrius region. Made by the creator of the Complete FireRed Upgrade engine, this hack uses the engine to its maximum potential. There are crazy field effects in the gyms and battle scenarios that you would not expect in a Pokémon game. The soundtrack of this game is amazing. I still find myself listening to the tracks while I'm walking from place to place.

This run was completed on the Difficult difficulty.

Rom Hack 22: Run & Bun

Author: dekzeh

Run & Bun is the hardest hack I've played to date. Each battle is filled with synergy and really shows the potential of each Pokémon. This game has a focus on resource management. Heart Scales are limited, Rare Candies are limited, and Berries are limited. I despise that items can be removed by Knock Off and some of the late game field effects are ridiculous. Permanent Magma Storm is a little ridiculous.

The AI has been buffed to take the player's speed and damage into account to see fast and slow kills on Pokémon, though it does not understand type switches on a Mega. The Elite 4 is also really unique. You get to choose between Single and Double Battle variants for each trainer, but you must do 2 of each.

If you are going to play this game, do not play it blind. I would have had much more fun if I played with the documentation.

My Mega of choice was Altaria because it made Drake easier to deal with.

Rom Hack 24: Garbage Gold

Author: sauceyaTTa

The word "garbage" in this hack does not describe the hack itself, rather it describes the quality of Pokémon you are able to encounter. You are limited to Pokémon that don't fully evolve, or have very low base stats. Even with this restriction, the game still has you face gauntlets of trainers without healing, and Mega-Evolved Pokémon in the late game. It's not actually too bad though. Learnsets have been modified to make the game more balanced than I made it sound just now. The difficulty curve is not that bad, save for Whitney. She caused a lot of trouble during my playthrough.

I found a few Pokémon to be extremely helpful. Alolan Dugtrio feels like you get to use an Excadrill in this hack. Hisuian Zoroark is straight up broken, I think it's not supposed to be obtainable. Gurdurr with guts becomes an absolute demon in the Elite 4. If you look hard enough, there will be some gems in this hack.

There's also a completely new story where Johto won a war against Kanto. The story actually explains why the Pokémon you encounter are not great.
